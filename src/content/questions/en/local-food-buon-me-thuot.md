Buon Me Thuot's food is Central Highlands cuisine — different from Hanoi's restraint or Saigon's sweetness. It's hearty, herb-forward, and tied to the land.

**Must-try dishes**

**Thit nai (venison)**
- Farmed deer from Lak Lake area
- Lean, slightly gamey, more iron-rich than beef
- Preparations: grilled (nuong), hotpot (lau), stir-fried with lemongrass (xao xa)
- Price: 150,000-300,000 VND/dish (serves 2-3)
- Where: Restaurants along Le Duan, near central market

**Com lam (bamboo rice)**
- Rice steamed inside bamboo tubes over open fire
- Takes on subtle bamboo fragrance
- Served with grilled pork or fish
- Price: 30,000-50,000 VND/tube
- Where: Street stalls, ethnic minority restaurants

**Goi la (herb salad)**
- 30-50 different herbs and leaves
- Served with grilled pork (thit nuong)
- Wrap in rice paper, dip in mam nem (fermented fish sauce)
- Price: 80,000-150,000 VND
- Where: Specialty Tay Nguyen restaurants

**Ruou can (bamboo-tube wine)**
- Rice wine fermented in large clay jars
- Drunk through long bamboo straws
- Communal — multiple people share one jar
- Alcohol content: 15-25%
- Price: 100,000-200,000 VND/jar
- Where: Ethnic restaurants, festivals

**Other local specialties**

| Dish | Description | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Bo mot ngay | "One-day beef" — marinated, dried beef | 120k-200k VND |
| Ga noi vuon | Free-range chicken, grilled or steamed | 150k-250k VND |
| Ca song da | Grilled fish with herbs | 100k-180k VND |
| Muoi kien vang | Yellow ant salt — condiment for meat | 30k-50k VND/jar |
| Bo kho | Beef stew with lemongrass, spicy | 80k-120k VND |

**Where to eat**

**Budget (50,000-100,000 VND/person)**
- Street stalls near central market
- Com binh dan (rice with dishes) shops
- Banh mi and pho stands

**Mid-range (100,000-200,000 VND/person)**
- Restaurants on Le Duan Boulevard
- Family-run spots near the market
- Places with "dac san Tay Nguyen" signage

**Higher-end (200,000-400,000 VND/person)**
- Hotel restaurants
- Tourist-oriented venues with gong performances
- Air-conditioned venues with English menus

**Dining etiquette**

- Wait to be seated — don't just grab a table
- Rice is often free and refillable
- Tipping not expected but appreciated (5-10%)
- Ask for mam nem (fish sauce) if not brought to table
- Shared plates are normal — order 1 dish per 2 people

**Dietary considerations**

- Vegetarian options limited — pho chay available at some stalls
- Buddhist temples serve vegetarian food on 1st/15th lunar days
- Allergies: communicate clearly (translate app helps)
- Halal: very limited options — ask at the mosque
