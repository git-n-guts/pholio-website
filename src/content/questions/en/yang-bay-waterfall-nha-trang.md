Yang Bay is a developed waterfall resort west of Nha Trang — more commercialized than wild waterfalls but with better facilities and family-friendly activities.

## Overview

**Location:** 45 km west of Nha Trang (Khánh Sơn mountain area)

**Type:** Developed waterfall resort (not wild nature)

**Best for:** Families, groups, travelers wanting easy nature day

**Time needed:** Half-day to full-day (4–6 hours)

**Entry fee:** 100,000–150,000 VND (includes shuttle from gate to falls)

**Open:** Daily, 7:00am–5:00pm

## Getting There

### Organized Tour (Easiest)

**Price:** 300,000–400,000 VND per person

**Includes:**
- Round-trip transport from Nha Trang hotel
- Entry fee
- Guide
- Sometimes lunch

**Schedule:** Pick-up 7–8am, return 2–4pm

**Booking:** Through hotels, travel agencies on Nguyễn Thiện Thuật

**Advantages:**
- No planning needed
- Guaranteed transport
- English-speaking guide
- Meet other travelers

**Disadvantages:**
- Fixed schedule (less flexibility)
- May include shopping stops
- Less authentic

### Local Bus + Taxi (Budget)

**Step 1: Bus to Khánh Sơn**
- Departure: Nha Trang bus station
- Price: 50,000 VND
- Time: 1 hour
- Frequency: Every 30–60 minutes

**Step 2: Taxi or Xe Ôm to Yang Bay**
- Price: 100,000–150,000 VND (negotiate round-trip)
- Time: 15–20 minutes
- Arrange pickup time in advance

**Total cost:** ~200,000 VND per person (cheaper for couples)

**Advantages:**
- Cheap
- Flexible timing

**Disadvantages:**
- Multiple transfers
- Dependent on bus schedule
- Need to arrange return transport

### Motorbike (Adventurous)

**Route:** Nha Trang → Highway 1A → turn west at Diên Khánh → follow signs to Yang Bay

**Distance:** 45 km

**Time:** 1–1.5 hours each way

**Road condition:** Paved highway, then mountain road (good condition)

**Parking:** Free at Yang Bay

**Cost:** 100k–150k VND rental + 50k VND fuel

**Advantages:**
- Complete flexibility
- Stop for photos
- Cheap for solo travelers

**Disadvantages:**
- Navigate yourself
- Mountain roads (winding)
- No guide

### Private Taxi

**Price:** 600,000–800,000 VND round-trip (wait 2–3 hours)

**Capacity:** 4 passengers

**Best for:** Families, groups, travelers who value comfort

**Booking:** Through hotels or Mai Linh/Vinasun

## What's at Yang Bay

### Waterfalls

**Yang Bay Main Falls:**
- Height: ~10 meters
- Pool: Large natural swimming area
- Access: Shuttle from entrance + short walk
- Facilities: Changing rooms, lockers, life jackets available

**Yang Khang Falls:**
- Smaller, less crowded
- Requires additional hiking (15–20 min)
- More natural setting
- Good for photos

### Swimming Pools

**Natural pools:**
- Fed by waterfall
- Designated swimming zones
- Life jackets available (free or small deposit)
- Safe for children (shallow areas)

**Man-made pools:**
- Chlorinated pool (alternative to natural)
- Changing rooms and showers available

### Animal Attractions

**Elephant rides:**
- Price: 100,000–200,000 VND per ride (5–10 min)
- Location: Near entrance area
- Ethical note: Captive elephants for tourism

**Crocodile show:**
- Schedule: Multiple shows daily (check at entrance)
- Price: Included in entry or small extra fee
- Features: Keeper handles crocodiles, photo ops

**Fish massage:**
- Price: 30,000–50,000 VND per session
- Experience: Fish nibble dead skin from feet
- Duration: 15–20 minutes

### Other Facilities

**Picnic areas:**
- Tables and benches
- Some shaded areas
- Bring your own food or buy on-site

**Restaurant:**
- Vietnamese food (phở, cơm, grilled items)
- Price: 80,000–150,000 VND per meal
- Quality: Average, overpriced for what you get

**Gift shop:**
- Souvenirs, snacks, drinks
- Price: Higher than city

## Sample Visit Timeline

**7:30am:** Depart Nha Trang (tour or motorbike)

**8:30–9:00am:** Arrive Yang Bay, pay entry fee

**9:00–9:30am:** Shuttle to waterfall area

**9:30–11:00am:** Swim at Yang Bay Falls, take photos

**11:00–11:30am:** Walk to Yang Khang Falls (optional)

**11:30am–12:30pm:** Lunch (bring picnic or buy at restaurant)

**12:30–1:30pm:** Animal attractions (elephant, crocodile show, fish massage)

**1:30–2:00pm:** Shuttle back to entrance, depart

**3:00pm:** Arrive back Nha Trang

**Total time:** ~7 hours (including travel)

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Family-friendly:** Safe swimming areas, animal attractions for kids
- **Good facilities:** Changing rooms, lockers, life jackets, restaurant
- **Easy access:** No serious hiking required
- **Multiple activities:** Waterfalls, swimming, animals, shows
- **Year-round:** Open even in rainy season (though less ideal)

### Cons

- **Commercialized:** Not a wild nature experience
- **Entry fee:** 100k–150k VND is steep for Vietnam
- **Animal ethics:** Captive elephants, crocodile shows
- **Crowded:** Especially weekends and holidays
- **Overpriced food:** Restaurant quality doesn't match price

## Tips for a Good Visit

**Before you go:**
- Check tour inclusions (some skip entry fee)
- Bring swimsuit and towel
- Pack sunscreen and insect repellent
- Bring cash (no cards accepted)

**At the park:**
- Arrive early (before 9am) to avoid tour groups
- Use locker for valuables
- Confirm shuttle schedule (usually every 15–20 min)
- Check animal show times at entrance

**Money-saving tips:**
- Bring your own food and water
- Skip paid animal attractions if on budget
- Go by motorbike instead of tour (solo travelers)
- Share taxi cost with other travelers

## Comparison: Yang Bay vs Ba Hồ

| Feature | Yang Bay | Ba Hồ |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Distance** | 45 km | 25 km |
| **Entry fee** | 100k–150k VND | 20k–30k VND |
| **Facilities** | Developed (showers, restaurant) | Minimal (basic toilets) |
| **Hiking** | Easy (short walks) | Moderate (1.5 km trail) |
| **Best for** | Families, groups | Adventurous, nature lovers |
| **Crowds** | Often crowded | Quieter |
| **Swimming** | Safe, designated areas | Natural, unsupervised |

## The verdict

Yang Bay is a good option for families and travelers wanting an easy, facility-equipped waterfall day trip from Nha Trang. It's commercialized and pricier than wild alternatives, but offers safe swimming and multiple activities.

**Best for:** Families with children, groups, travelers wanting comfort, first-time waterfall visitors
**Skip if:** Seeking wild nature, budget travelers, experienced hikers (try Ba Hồ instead)
