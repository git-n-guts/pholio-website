Buying coffee in Buon Me Thuot is cheaper and better than anywhere else in Vietnam — if you know where to look and what to ask.

**Where to buy**

**Central market (Cho Buon Ma Thuot) — Best overall**
- Green beans: 50,000-80,000 VND/kg
- Roasted beans: 100,000-200,000 VND/kg
- Specialty lots: 150,000-400,000 VND/kg
- Bargaining expected — start at 70% of asking price
- Vacuum sealing available (free or 5,000-10,000 VND)

**Plantations (during harvest Nov-Jan)**
- Green beans: 40,000-60,000 VND/kg (farm-gate)
- Roasted: 80,000-150,000 VND/kg
- Freshest option, direct from producer
- Requires transport and advance arrangement

**Trung Nguyen flagship**
- Packaged beans: 150,000-500,000 VND/kg
- Premium branding, gift packaging
- Consistent quality, higher prices
- Good for gifts, not daily drinking

**Roadside stalls**
- Some sell house-roasted beans
- Prices vary widely (80,000-200,000 VND/kg)
- Taste before buying bulk
- No packaging — bring your own bags

**Avoid**

- **Airport gift shops**: 200-300% markup, stale beans
- **Hotel gift shops**: Same beans, higher prices
- **Pre-packaged "gift sets"**: Unknown roast date, high markup
- **Weasel coffee under 1M VND/100g**: Fake or caged

**What to buy**

| Type | Price/kg | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Robusta green | 50k-80k VND | Home roasters, long storage |
| Robusta roasted | 100k-200k VND | Daily drinking, phin filter |
| Blend (70/30) | 120k-250k VND | Balanced flavor |
| Arabica (Da Lat) | 200k-400k VND | Black coffee, pour-over |
| Honey-processed | 250k-500k VND | Specialty, gifts |

**Questions to ask**

- "Rang hom nao?" (When was it roasted?) — ideal: within 2 weeks
- "Loai gi?" (What type?) — Robusta, Arabica, or blend
- "Co xay khong?" (Can you grind it?) — most vendors will grind free
- "Goi chan khong?" (Can you vacuum-seal?) — extends freshness

**How much to buy**

- Personal use: 1-2kg (lasts 1-2 months)
- Gifts: 500g per person (split into smaller bags)
- Coffee enthusiast: 5-10kg (green beans store well)

**Storage**

- Green beans: Cool, dry place, 6-12 months
- Roasted beans: Airtight container, 2-4 weeks peak freshness
- Avoid: Direct sunlight, heat, moisture
- Don't freeze — causes condensation when thawed
