Nha Trang is Vietnam's main coastal city for seafood — the South China Sea catch arrives daily at the docks. The question isn't whether the seafood is good. It's whether you're paying a fair price for it. The gap between the tourist rate and the local rate is wide enough here that where you eat matters more than what you order.

## The Chợ Đầm market system

The most cost-effective way to eat seafood in Nha Trang is the market-to-table system at **Chợ Đầm** (Trần Quý Cáp, open 6am–6pm). Walk into the seafood section, choose live prawns, crabs, squid, clams, or fish from the tanks, pay by the kilogram, then carry your purchase to one of the small cooking stalls around the market's perimeter. The stalls charge a flat fee per preparation — steamed (hấp), grilled with salt and pepper (nướng muối ớt), or stir-fried with butter and garlic (xào bơ tỏi) — typically 30,000–60,000 VND per dish.

A full meal for two — two seafood dishes, morning glory stir-fry, rice, and cold beers — runs 400,000–650,000 VND total. That's the local standard.

The one rule: agree on prices before anything enters the tank or the kitchen. Both the seafood vendors and the cooking stalls expect this and will answer a direct question about per-kilo costs.

## Sit-down restaurants: the Bietthuy cluster

For a conventional restaurant with table service at honest prices, **Đường Bietthuy** — a quiet street near Chợ Đầm — has a cluster of seafood restaurants with live tanks out front. Mostly Vietnamese families, menus in Vietnamese with photos, staff accustomed to tourists who point at what they want. Prices run 30–50% lower than the beachfront strip.

**Yến Phi** (Hùng Vương, near the market) is a step up in ambiance — proper seating, faster service, and a clientele of Vietnamese professionals on lunch breaks. Crab hotpot (lẩu ghẹ) at around 350,000–450,000 VND for two is the standout order.

## Louisiane Brewhouse — when the setting matters

**Louisiane Brewhouse** (29 Trần Phú) is the exception to the rule that beachfront restaurants are overpriced. The seafood is carefully sourced, the beer is brewed on-site, and the setup on the beach road is the best of any sit-down restaurant in the city. Expect 700,000–1,200,000 VND for two with drinks.

It's the right choice for a first or last evening, or when comfort and a view take priority over value. The rest of the Trần Phú strip doesn't offer the same quality at similar prices.

## What to order

**Tôm hùm** (lobster): Nha Trang has an active lobster farming industry. Prices vary by season — 400,000–600,000 VND per small lobster at market, more at restaurants. Worth the splurge once.

**Ghẹ** (blue swimmer crab): order steamed or in hotpot, by weight. Most restaurants have them live.

**Mực** (squid): grilled whole with salt, pepper, and lime is the default and consistently good.

**Ốc** (snails): Nha Trang has a strong ốc street food scene. Evening stalls near the night market on Trần Phú serve half a dozen varieties with dipping sauces.

## The scam pattern to know

Some tourist-facing restaurants don't post prices per kilogram and reveal the total only when the bill arrives. The fix is simple: ask *"bao nhiêu một ký?"* (how much per kilogram?) before anything goes in the tank or the kitchen. Any restaurant that can't or won't answer that question directly is one to skip.

This is different from aggressive upselling — it's a structural setup at certain establishments specifically targeting customers who don't ask. Once you ask the question, it stops being an issue.
