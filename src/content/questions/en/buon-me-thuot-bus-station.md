Buon Me Thuot's bus network connects the city to major destinations across Vietnam's Central Highlands and beyond. Understanding the station layout and booking options makes travel smoother.

**Two bus stations**

**Interprovincial Bus Station** (Ben Xe Lien Tinh):
- **Location**: QL14, 5km north of city center
- **Routes**: Long-distance to HCMC, Hanoi, Nha Trang, Da Lat, Pleiku, Kon Tum
- **Operators**: Phuong Trang, Hoang Long, Thanh Binh, The Sinh Tourist
- **Facilities**: Ticket counters, waiting area, toilets, food stalls, luggage storage

**Local Bus Station** (Ben Xe Noi Tinh):
- **Location**: City center (Le Duan Street)
- **Routes**: Provincial towns, districts within Dak Lak
- **Operators**: Local minibuses, shared taxis
- **Facilities**: Basic (ticket booths, informal seating)

**Getting to Interprovincial Station**

**From city center**:

| Method | Cost | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Grab/GrabBike | 50k-100k VND | 10 min | Book via app, fixed price |
| Taxi | 60k-120k VND | 10 min | Negotiate or meter |
| Motorbike taxi | 30k-50k VND | 10 min | Informal, negotiate first |
| Public bus | 10k VND | 20 min | Route 01 or 03, frequent |
| Hotel shuttle | Free-50k VND | 10 min | Ask at reception |

**From Buon Ma Thuot Airport** (BMV):
- **Distance**: 8km to bus station
- **Taxi**: 80k-150k VND, 15 minutes
- **Grab**: Available, similar pricing
- **Note**: No direct public bus from airport

**Bus types**

**Sleeper buses** (most common for long-distance):
- **Configuration**: 40-45 berths in 3 levels
- **Recline**: ~45 degrees (not fully flat)
- **Amenities**: Blanket, water, sometimes snack
- **Best for**: Overnight trips, journeys over 4 hours

**Seated buses**:
- **Configuration**: 45-50 seats
- **Recline**: Standard bus seats
- **Amenities**: Sometimes AC, water
- **Best for**: Day trips under 4 hours

**Limousine vans**:
- **Configuration**: 9-11 luxury seats
- **Recline**: Full recline available
- **Amenities**: AC, WiFi, charging ports, water
- **Best for**: Comfort seekers, shorter routes

**Booking tickets**

**Online** (recommended):
- **futa.vn**: Phuong Trang official site, English available
- **12go.asia**: Aggregator, multiple operators, English
- **Vexere.com**: Vietnamese site, good for local operators
- **Advance**: 1-7 days for normal periods, 1-2 weeks for holidays

**At station**:
- **Counters**: 10+ ticket counters, compare prices
- **Language**: Vietnamese only at most counters
- **Payment**: Cash only (VND)
- **Advance**: Same-day to 30 days ahead

**Via hotel/guesthouse**:
- **Service**: Most hotels book for guests
- **Fee**: 10k-50k VND commission (sometimes free)
- **Convenience**: High (delivered to hotel)
- **Payment**: Cash or add to room bill

**Station facilities**

**Interprovincial Station**:
- **Waiting area**: Covered seating, fans (limited AC)
- **Toilets**: 5k-10k VND (bring small change)
- **Food**: Stalls inside and outside station
- **Luggage**: Storage available (20k-50k VND/bag)
- **ATMs**: 2-3 machines inside station
- **Shops**: Convenience stores, phone cards, snacks

**Local Station**:
- **Waiting area**: Minimal (outdoor covered)
- **Toilets**: Basic, sometimes free
- **Food**: Street vendors outside
- **Luggage**: On bus only (no storage)

**Peak times and holidays**

**High demand periods** (book 1-2 weeks ahead):
- **Tet (Lunar New Year)**: Late January-early February
- **Hung Kings Commemoration**: April (10th day, 3rd lunar month)
- **Reunification Day**: April 30 - May 1
- **Summer**: June-August (family travel)

**Normal periods**:
- Same-day tickets usually available
- Best to book 1-2 days ahead for sleepers
- Walk-up fare sometimes higher than advance

**Safety and security**

**At station**:
- **Pickpockets**: Watch bags in crowded areas
- **Scams**: Buy tickets at counters only (not from touts)
- **Luggage**: Keep valuables in carry-on

**On bus**:
- **Valuables**: Keep phone, wallet, passport with you
- **Sleep**: Loop bag strap around arm/leg
- **Shoes**: Keep on or tied to bag (theft common)
