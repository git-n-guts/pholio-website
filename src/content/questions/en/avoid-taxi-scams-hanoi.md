Taxi fraud in Hanoi is a documented pattern, not an isolated incident. The city's tourist zones — around Hoàn Kiếm Lake, Nội Bài airport, and Lăng Bác — generate the highest concentration of taxi-related complaints. Understanding the scams is the fastest way to avoid them.

## The scam types

**Rigged meter (the most common)**: The taxi looks legitimate — meter present, company name on the door. The meter runs at double or triple the normal rate, calibrated to look like legitimate ticking. By the time you arrive, the fare is wildly inconsistent with the distance traveled. Harder to detect than it sounds; the meter looks normal, just fast.

**The long route**: The driver takes a 12km route to a destination that's 4km away. If you don't know Hanoi's layout or aren't watching the map, you won't notice until the fare is already high.

**The airport tout**: Inside Nội Bài arrivals, people approach new arrivals offering 'official transport' with a fixed price. The price sounds reasonable. It isn't — and the final bill involves invented surcharges. These are not official anything.

**The xe ôm overcharge**: A motorbike driver quotes one price verbally, doesn't confirm it's agreed, and demands more at the destination. Standard preventive measure: agree on the price explicitly before boarding, and confirm it again at the destination before dismounting.

## If you're in a metered taxi

- **Request the meter be turned on before moving** — if refused, exit
- **Track the route on Google Maps** on your phone as you travel
- **Normal rate**: 13,000–16,000 VND/km for standard car taxis in Hanoi (2026)
- **Airport rate**: slightly higher starting fee at Nội Bài — expect 280,000–380,000 VND total to Old Quarter at normal rates
- **Know your route**: Old Quarter to Nội Bài airport is approximately 30km — a 240,000–300,000 VND trip at normal rates
