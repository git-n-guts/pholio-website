Traveling from Nha Trang to Đà Lạt is a popular route — you're going from the coast to the Central Highlands, gaining 1,500 meters in elevation along one of Vietnam's most scenic mountain passes.

## Bus options

### Sleeper Bus (Standard)

**Companies:** Phương Trang, Thành Bưởi, The Sinh Tourist

**Price:** 130,000–160,000 VND ($5–6.50 USD)

**Travel time:** 3.5–4 hours

**Schedule:** Departures every 30–60 minutes from 5:00am to 3:00pm

**Comfort:** Reclining sleeper berths (lie flat), air-conditioned, onboard water

**Capacity:** 40–44 passengers

**Best for:** Budget travelers, backpackers

**Departure points in Nha Trang:**
- Phương Trang: 209 Nguyễn Thiện Thuật
- Thành Bưởi: 37 Nguyễn Thiện Thuật
- The Sinh Tourist: 28A Nguyễn Thiện Thuật

### Limousine Van (Premium)

**Companies:** Various (often booked through hotels)

**Price:** 200,000–250,000 VND ($8–10 USD)

**Travel time:** 3–3.5 hours (slightly faster, fewer stops)

**Schedule:** On-demand or fixed departures (9am, 1pm, 3pm)

**Comfort:** 9-seater Mercedes Sprinter, leather seats, more legroom, USB charging

**Capacity:** 9 passengers

**Best for:** Couples, travelers who value comfort, motion-sickness-prone passengers (fewer stops = less nausea)

## The route: Khánh Lê Mountain Pass

**Distance:** 130 kilometers

**Elevation gain:** From sea level (Nha Trang) to 1,500m (Đà Lạt)

**Road condition:** Paved, two lanes, well-maintained

**Scenery:** Coastal plains → tropical forest → mountain云雾 → pine highlands

**The pass:**
- 30 kilometers of winding mountain roads
- Multiple hairpin turns ("cua tay áo")
- Scenic viewpoints at the top
- Rest stop with bathrooms and photo ops

**Motion sickness warning:** This is one of the most winding routes in Vietnam. If you're prone to carsickness:
- Take motion sickness pills 30 minutes before departure
- Sit in the front of the bus (less sway)
- Look forward, not at your phone
- Choose a limousine van (fewer stops, smoother ride)

## Booking your ticket

### In person (recommended)

**Where:** Travel agencies on Nguyễn Thiện Thuật street (backpacker area)

**How:** Walk in, show your desired date, pay cash

**Advantage:** Can compare prices and schedules across multiple counters

**Tip:** Book 1–2 days in advance during peak season (December–February, June–August)

### Through your hotel

**How:** Ask reception to book for you

**Price:** Same as counter (no markup) or +10k VND service fee

**Advantage:** Hotel staff handles everything, sometimes includes pickup from your door

**Disadvantage:** Limited company options

### Online (for foreigners)

**Websites:** 12go.asia, Baolau.com

**Price:** 150,000–180,000 VND (slight markup for convenience)

**Advantage:** Book before you arrive, English interface

**Disadvantage:** Need to print ticket or show QR code at counter

## What to expect on the bus

**Before departure:**
- Arrive 15 minutes early
- Show ticket (paper or phone)
- Staff assigns you a berth number
- Store large backpacks in luggage compartment

**Onboard:**
- Remove shoes before entering sleeper area
- Lie down, pull blanket over legs
- Keep valuables with you (small bag under head)
- Bus is air-conditioned — can be cold

**Rest stop (midway):**
- 10–15 minute break
- Bathrooms (10,000 VND fee, have small change)
- Snacks, coffee, souvenirs
- Photo opportunity of mountain view

**Arrival in Đà Lạt:**
- Bus drops at company station
- Taxis and xe ôm wait outside
- Some hotels offer pickup if arranged in advance

## Alternative: Private taxi

**Price:** 1,200,000–1,500,000 VND ($48–60 USD) per car

**Capacity:** 4 passengers

**Best for:** Families, groups, travelers with lots of luggage

**Advantage:** Door-to-door, flexible departure time, can stop for photos

**Booking:** Through hotels or taxi companies (Mai Linh, Vinasun)

## The verdict

The bus from Nha Trang to Đà Lạt is affordable, frequent, and scenic. Choose a sleeper bus for budget travel or a limousine van for comfort.

**Best for:** All travelers — this is the standard route
**Tip:** Take motion sickness pills if sensitive, and bring a jacket for the mountain rest stop
