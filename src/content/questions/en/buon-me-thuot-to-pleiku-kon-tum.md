The route from Buon Me Thuot north to Pleiku and Kon Tum follows QL14 through the heart of Vietnam's coffee country — rolling hills covered in coffee plants, rubber plantations, and occasional ethnic minority villages.

**Buon Me Thuot to Pleiku**

**Distance and route**:
- **Distance**: 180km via QL14
- **Time**: 3-4 hours (bus), 4-5 hours (motorbike)
- **Route**: QL14 northwest through Ea H'leo, Krong Buk, then into Gia Lai province
- **Terrain**: Rolling hills, mostly flat, no major passes

**Bus options**:

| Operator | Type | Price | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hoang Long | Seated | 200k-250k VND | Hourly 5am-5pm |
| Phuong Trang | Sleeper | 250k-300k VND | 6am, 7am, 8pm |
| Local minibus | Seated | 180k-220k VND | When full (5am-4pm) |

**Booking**:
- **At station**: Buon Me Thuot bus station, no advance booking needed for day buses
- **Online**: 12go.asia (limited availability), futa.vn (Phuong Trang only)
- **Travel agencies**: Most guesthouses can book for small fee (10k-20k VND)

**Bus stations**:
- **Departure**: Buon Me Thuot Interprovincial Bus Station (central)
- **Arrival**: Pleiku Interprovincial Station (3km from center)

**Motorbike option**:

**Route highlights**:
- Coffee plantations stretch for 100km+
- Pass through Ea H'leo town (fuel, food)
- Gia Lai province welcome sign (photo opportunity)
- Final approach into Pleiku via Tran Hung Dao

**Road conditions**:
- QL14: Paved, good condition throughout
- Traffic: Moderate truck traffic, occasional buses
- Shoulders: Narrow in sections, caution when passing

**Safety notes**:
- Fuel up in Buon Me Thuot or Ea H'leo (fewer stations mid-route)
- Watch for trucks pulling out unexpectedly
- Carry cash (ATMs sparse outside towns)

**Pleiku to Kon Tum**

**Distance and route**:
- **Distance**: 60km
- **Time**: 1.5 hours (bus), 2 hours (motorbike)
- **Route**: Continue on QL14 north through forest

**Bus options**:
- **Frequency**: Every 30-60 minutes 5am-6pm
- **Price**: 100k-150k VND
- **Operators**: Same as Buon Me Thuot-Pleiku route

**Direct Buon Me Thuot-Kon Tum buses**:
- Some buses continue from Buon Me Thuot to Kon Tum (4-5 hours total)
- Change at Pleiku if no direct connection available
- Kon Tum is terminal for many northbound buses

**Motorbike option** (Buon Me Thuot to Kon Tum direct):

**Total distance**: 240km
**Total time**: 5-6 hours with breaks
**Recommended**: Split into 2 days with overnight in Pleiku

**Why stop in Pleiku**:
- Breaks up long ride
- Explore Pleiku attractions (Bien Ho lake, Min Thang pagoda)
- Reduces fatigue-related risks
- More accommodation options than mid-route towns

**Private car/taxi**:

**Buon Me Thuot to Pleiku**:
- **Cost**: 1,2M-1,8M VND
- **Time**: 3 hours
- **Booking**: Grab (long-distance), local taxi, hotel

**Buon Me Thuot to Kon Tum**:
- **Cost**: 1,8M-2,5M VND
- **Time**: 4-5 hours
- **Note**: May require advance booking (fewer cars willing)

**No train options**

- **Railway**: No rail connection in Central Highlands
- **Nearest station**: Nha Trang (coast, 4-5 hours from BMT)
- **Alternative**: Fly to Pleiku (limited flights from Hanoi/HCMC only)

**Border checkpoints**

- No international borders
- Occasional police checkpoints for vehicle registration
- Carry driver's license and vehicle papers (if riding)

**Best time to travel**

**Dry season (November-April)**:
- Clear skies, good visibility
- Comfortable temperatures (20-28°C)
- Best for motorbike

**Rainy season (May-October)**:
- Afternoon showers common
- Road can be slick (especially painted markings)
- Bus recommended

**Attractions along the route**

**Ea H'leo** (halfway BMT-Pleiku):
- Coffee stops with plantation views
- Local restaurants (com tam, pho)
- Fuel stations

**Bien Ho** (Pleiku):
- Volcanic crater lake
- Temple on island
- Worth 1-2 hour detour

**Kon Tum highlights** (if continuing):
- Wooden Church (Nha tho Go)
- Suspension bridge
- Ethnic minority markets
