Cam Ranh International Airport (CXR) sits 35 kilometers south of Nha Trang city center — farther than it looks on a map because the road runs along the peninsula. The transfer takes 40–65 minutes depending on transport choice and traffic. You have four realistic options, from cheapest to most expensive.

## Option 1: Public bus 19 — 70,000 VND

Bus 19 runs between Cam Ranh airport and Nha Trang along the coastal road, dropping passengers on Trần Phú (the main beachfront boulevard). The fare is 70,000 VND, paid on board. Buses run roughly every 30–45 minutes from early morning until around 8pm.

The bus stops at the terminal exit, not inside the airport building. Walk past the taxi touts to the road-side stop. Journey time: 50–65 minutes with stops.

This is the right choice if you're traveling light, your hotel is within walking distance of Trần Phú, and you're arriving outside peak times.

## Option 2: Shuttle mini-bus — 100,000–150,000 VND

Several operators run shared shuttle vans between the airport and hotels along the beach road. Book at the arrivals counter inside the terminal (look for booths after customs), or arrange in advance through your hotel. Fare: 100,000–150,000 VND per person.

Shuttles typically wait until they have enough passengers to depart (usually 15–30 minutes after major flight arrivals), then do a round of hotel drop-offs along Trần Phú. If your hotel is in the city center rather than on the beach road, confirm the drop point before paying.

## Option 3: Grab — 280,000–380,000 VND

Open the Grab app before you leave the arrivals building and set your destination. Walk to the designated pickup zone outside the terminal. Fare to the city center runs 280,000–380,000 VND. Price is transparent before you confirm, and you pay by app or exact cash — no negotiation required.

One note: Grab drivers at Cam Ranh sometimes call to ask you to come to them rather than waiting at the pickup spot. Follow the pin in the app, not the call — drivers occasionally park outside the official zone.

## Option 4: Metered taxi — 350,000–450,000 VND

**Mai Linh** (green cars) and **Vinasun** (white cars) run metered taxis from the official rank outside arrivals. Fare to Nha Trang city: 350,000–450,000 VND depending on exact destination. Slightly more expensive than Grab and less transparent, but fine if the meter is running.

The phrase you need: *"bật đồng hồ"* — turn on the meter. Ask before the car moves. A driver who refuses or quotes a round number above 500,000 VND is not taking the metered route.

## The Cam Ranh taxi scam — what it looks like

Unlicensed taxis work the arrivals exit at Cam Ranh. They approach assertively, sometimes holding a printed sign, and quote flat rates that sound reasonable — until you're in the car and the number doubles, or a "surcharge" appears. This is the single most-reported complaint on TripAdvisor Nha Trang over the last five years, especially for late-night arrivals.

The fix is simple: only use the three options above. If an unmarked driver approaches you inside or immediately outside arrivals, walk past to the official taxi rank or the Grab pickup zone.
