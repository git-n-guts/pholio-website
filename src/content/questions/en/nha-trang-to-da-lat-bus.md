Taking the bus from Nha Trang to Đà Lạt is the most popular and budget-friendly option. Here's everything you need to know.

## Bus Companies

### Phương Trang (Futa Bus)

**Price:** 140,000–160,000 VND one-way

**Vehicle type:** Sleeper bus (40–44 berths)

**Schedule:** Every 30–60 minutes from 5:00am to 3:00pm

**Departure point:** 209 Nguyễn Thiện Thuật, Nha Trang

**Arrival point:** Phương Trang Station, 292 Hoàng Văn Thụ, Đà Lạt

**Features:**
- Air-conditioned
- Reclining sleeper berths
- Onboard water
- Blanket provided
- Luggage compartment

**Booking:** Counter, hotel, or online (12go.asia, Baolau.com)

### Thành Bưởi

**Price:** 130,000–150,000 VND one-way

**Vehicle type:** Sleeper bus (40–44 berths)

**Schedule:** Every 30–60 minutes from 5:00am to 3:00pm

**Departure point:** 37 Nguyễn Thiện Thuật, Nha Trang

**Arrival point:** Thành Bưởi Station, Đà Lạt

**Features:**
- Similar to Phương Trang
- Slightly cheaper
- Reliable service

**Booking:** Counter or hotel

### The Sinh Tourist

**Price:** 150,000–170,000 VND one-way

**Vehicle type:** Sleeper bus

**Schedule:** Fixed departures (6am, 9am, 1pm, 3pm)

**Departure point:** 28A Nguyễn Thiện Thuật, Nha Trang

**Best for:** Backpackers, travelers who book multi-city tours

## Limousine Van (Premium Option)

**Companies:** Various (book through hotels)

**Price:** 200,000–250,000 VND one-way

**Vehicle type:** 9-seater Mercedes Sprinter

**Schedule:** On-demand or fixed departures (9am, 1pm, 3pm)

**Travel time:** 3–3.5 hours (slightly faster, fewer stops)

**Features:**
- Leather seats (more legroom)
- USB charging ports
- Complimentary water
- Less motion sickness (fewer stops)

**Best for:** Travelers who value comfort, motion-sickness-prone passengers

## The Route: Khánh Lê Mountain Pass

**Distance:** 130 kilometers

**Elevation gain:** From sea level to 1,500m

**Road condition:** Paved, two lanes, well-maintained

**Scenery:** Coastal plains → tropical forest → mountain云雾 → pine highlands

**The pass:**
- 30 kilometers of winding mountain roads
- Multiple hairpin turns ("cua tay áo")
- Scenic viewpoints at the top
- Rest stop with bathrooms and photo ops

**Motion sickness warning:** This is one of the most winding routes in Vietnam. If you're prone to carsickness:
- Take motion sickness pills 30 minutes before departure
- Sit in the front of the bus (less sway)
- Look forward, not at your phone
- Consider limousine van (fewer stops, smoother ride)

## Booking Your Ticket

### In Person (Recommended)

**Where:** Bus counters on Nguyễn Thiện Thuật street

**How:** Walk in, show desired date, pay cash

**Advantage:** Can compare prices and schedules, no booking fees

**Tip:** Book 1–2 days in advance during peak season (Dec–Feb, Jun–Aug)

### Through Hotel

**How:** Ask reception to book for you

**Price:** Same as counter (no markup) or +10k VND service fee

**Advantage:** Hotel staff handles everything, sometimes includes pickup

**Disadvantage:** Limited company options

### Online (for foreigners)

**Websites:** 12go.asia, Baolau.com

**Price:** 150,000–180,000 VND (slight markup for convenience)

**Advantage:** Book before arrival, English interface

**Disadvantage:** Need to print ticket or show QR code at counter

## What to Expect Onboard

### Before Departure

- Arrive 15 minutes early
- Show ticket (paper or phone)
- Staff assigns you a berth number
- Store large backpacks in luggage compartment

### Onboard

- Remove shoes before entering sleeper area
- Lie down, pull blanket over legs
- Keep valuables with you (small bag under head)
- Bus is air-conditioned — can be cold

### Rest Stop (Midway)

- 10–15 minute break at Khánh Lê pass summit
- Bathrooms (10,000 VND fee, have small change)
- Snacks, coffee, souvenirs
- Photo opportunity of mountain view

### Arrival in Đà Lạt

- Bus drops at company station
- Taxis and xe ôm wait outside
- Some hotels offer pickup if arranged in advance

## Tips for a Comfortable Journey

**Before boarding:**
- Use bathroom at departure point
- Buy water and snacks (bus stops are overpriced)
- Have small change for bathroom fee
- Download offline maps (no signal on pass)

**Onboard:**
- Keep jacket handy (AC can be cold)
- Use provided blanket
- Keep phone/camera accessible for photos at rest stop
- Don't put valuables in luggage compartment

**Motion sickness prevention:**
- Take pills 30 minutes before departure
- Sit in front berths (less sway)
- Look forward, not at phone
- Consider ginger candies

## The verdict

The bus from Nha Trang to Đà Lạt is affordable, frequent, and scenic. Phương Trang and Thành Bưởi are the most reliable companies. Book at the counter or through your hotel.

**Best for:** Budget travelers, most visitors
**Tip:** Take motion sickness pills if sensitive, and bring a jacket for the air-conditioned bus
