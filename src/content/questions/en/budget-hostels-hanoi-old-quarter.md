Hanoi's budget hostel market is competitive and well-reviewed. The Old Quarter has more beds per square kilometre than almost anywhere in Southeast Asia, ranging from party-first operations to quiet guesthouses in the same price bracket.

## What 150,000–280,000 VND gets you

At the lower end (150,000–190,000 VND): a bed in a 6–10 person dorm, shared or en-suite bathroom, air conditioning, WiFi, padlocked locker. Breakfast sometimes included.

At the upper end (220,000–280,000 VND): similar dorm, better mattress, en-suite bathroom, breakfast reliably included, common area with seating. The best-reviewed hostels in Hanoi cluster in this range.

## Specific picks (verified via reviews, 2026)

**Hanoi Backpackers Hostel** — around the Tạ Hiện / Hàng Buồm area. Long-running, high-volume, well-reviewed for clean facilities. Social atmosphere — evening bar on premises. 200,000–280,000 VND. Not quiet after 11pm.

**The Hub Hanoi** — Hàng Bạc area. Mid-Old Quarter location, slightly quieter than Tạ Hiện hostels. 170,000–240,000 VND. Breakfast included. Rooftop is a social space but not a party venue.

**Little Charm Hanoi Hostel** — Hàng Bông and surroundings. Quiet for a budget hostel — no bar on premises, family-run feel. 160,000–220,000 VND. Better for lighter sleepers.

**Vietnam Backpacker Hostels** (multiple locations) — chain operation with consistent standards. Tạ Hiện location is the party-leaning one; the Hàng Bè location is calmer. 190,000–260,000 VND.

**Note**: hostel quality and names change with ownership. Cross-reference the name with recent (last 3 months) Hostelworld or Booking.com reviews before confirming.

## How to choose

**Prioritize quiet:** stay away from hostels on or directly adjacent to Tạ Hiện. Look for hostels on Hàng Bông, Hàng Điếu, or the western Old Quarter streets.

**Prioritize social:** Tạ Hiện-area hostels are built for it. Hanoi Backpackers has the most consistent reputation for this.

**Prioritize value:** look at hostels with 100+ recent reviews and scores above 8.5 on Hostelworld. Hanoi's hostel market is honest about quality because the review volume keeps operators accountable.

## Booking

Peak season (October–April): book at least 7–10 days ahead for top-reviewed hostels. They fill. Shoulder and low season: 3–5 days ahead is usually enough. Hostelworld for the best dorm inventory; Booking.com also lists many Hanoi hostels with accurate photos.
