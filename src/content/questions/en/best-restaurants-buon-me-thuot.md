Buon Me Thuot doesn't have fine dining. It has honest, hearty restaurants serving Central Highlands specialties. This guide covers the reliable options.

**By cuisine type**

**Venison (thit nai)**
- **Nha Hang Thit Nai** (Le Duan Boulevard): Specializes in venison preparations — grilled, hotpot, stir-fry. 150,000-300,000 VND/dish. Air-conditioned, English menu.
- **Thit Nai Tay Nguyen** (near market): More casual, lower prices (120,000-250,000 VND). Local crowds.

**Central Highlands cuisine (goi la, com lam)**
- **Dac San Tay Nguyen** (Le Duan): Full menu of highland specialties. Goi la 80,000-150,000 VND. Com lam 30,000-50,000 VND. Tourist-friendly.
- **Nha Hang Ede** (city center): Ede ethnic minority cuisine. Ruou can available. Cultural performances on weekends (verify schedule).

**General Vietnamese**
- **Pho 68** (multiple locations): Reliable pho, 50,000-80,000 VND/bowl. Breakfast and lunch.
- **Com Binh Dan Co Ut** (near market): Rice with dishes, 40,000-60,000 VND. Local lunch spot.
- **Quan Nuong** (Le Duan): Grilled specialties — pork, beef, seafood. 100,000-200,000 VND/person.

**Hotel restaurants**
- **Muong Thanh Hotel**: Higher-end, international options. 200,000-500,000 VND/person.
- **Dakruco Hotel**: Central Highlands buffet. 150,000-300,000 VND.

**Street food areas**

- **Central market perimeter**: Breakfast stalls (5am-9am), com lam, banh mi
- **Le Duan Boulevard evening**: Grilled skewers, beer, casual dining
- **Nguyen Tat Thanh Street**: Late-night pho and hu tieu (after 10pm)

**Price ranges**

| Category | Per person | What to expect |
|---|---|---|
| Street food | 20k-50k VND | Plastic stools, no AC, cash only |
| Budget restaurant | 50k-100k VND | Fan or basic AC, Vietnamese menu |
| Mid-range | 100k-200k VND | AC, English menu, card accepted |
| Tourist venue | 200k-400k VND | Shows, air-con, gift shop attached |

**How to choose**

Look for:
- Local crowds (turnover = freshness)
- "Dac san Tay Nguyen" signage (Central Highlands specialties)
- Clean tables and fresh ingredients on display

Avoid:
- Touts outside restaurants (tourist traps)
- Pre-packaged "gift sets" (markup is high)
- Places with no locals (quality question)

**Payment**

- Cash preferred at street stalls and small restaurants
- Cards accepted at mid-range and hotel restaurants
- GrabPay/MoMo increasingly accepted
- Tipping not expected but 5-10% appreciated
